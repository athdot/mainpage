The Importance of Advertising


  Knowing how to advertise oneself is a very important skill in the workplace, as any veteran will tell you. The chance for someone to get hired goes up tenfold with even a little preparation, whether it be a resume, practice for interviews, elevator pitches and more. Being prepared for any situation and knowing just how to make yourself attractive to companies can mean the difference between working where you want when you want, or taking attrition in a job that you find yourself unmotivated and unhappy. Consequently, I took the liberty to write some of my own, down below.
On Elevator Pitches:

   An elevator pitch is quick. Something you can use, quite literally, to advertise yourself in the elevator going down before you leave to garner just the right attention and interest in just the right subject. Transitionally, planning one out is not only a good idea, its just common sense. While compiling one of my own, I found it interesting, trying to stuff a humans worth of content into just the span of only a minute, but in the same effort to compress, I also learned just what about myself makes me special, attractive, and a good choice for employers, while also outlining room to grow. Using an elevator pitch helps, and in the future it will be yet another tool in my toolset to get just the job I want.

Elevator pitch:

   "Hello! If you have a minute, I’d like to tell you a little about myself. Firstly though, Computer Science has always been a major passion of mine and I am deeply thankful to have the honor of being here today. My name is Charles Graham, and I am a Freshman at Purdue University, in Indiana - interested in Computer Science and working towards a major in that direction! As a person, I also would define myself as rather Investigative, Realistic, and Enterprising, and I’m always trying to use these skills to create, problem-solve, and lead in order to succeed. This could fit in Computer Science (Provided I get the opportunity) as my ability to create unique and efficient solutions are things that Computer Science cannot survive without. One particular tenant of life I always try to live by is to do my best. I also strive to be adaptable, and find myself able to fit into many situations, whether that be working with others or alone, taking charge or following, taking risks or playing it safe, and anything else that is required. Do you know what I can do to get connected?"
