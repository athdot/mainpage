Music:
  Music has always been a major part of my life, ever since I set my hands on a piano when I was young. Throughout my life I have always been creating, thinking, and expressing myself through the wonderful medium that is music. As I got older, I began to compose. Music in general has always had somewhat of a trancendental meaning for me, and in a way I've grown as a human being as a result. It wasnt until high school that I actually began to produce my own music and write.
  The below piece was co-written between myself and someone else, to commemerate the STEM school shooting, an active part of our community, and preaches a message of unity, solidarity, and common struggle with everything that happened. Musically, the background vocals are a wide range of people, which was a crazy logistical challenge within itself, as going to over 30 different classes over the span of 2 days to record people singing meant that myself and my team were constantly on the move. In the end, the finished product turned out perfectly, take a listen down below.

Difficult Roads.mp3

Computer Science, Coding, and More!


  Computer Science has long been a part of my life, and I even have my first code experience all the way back in 7th grade. I began with JavaScript, but throughout the years I have expanded, learned, and broadened my repitoir to contain things like C, C#, Java, JavaScript, A little Python, RobotC, and of course: HTML. In fact, you can even look up old code of mine on JSFiddle, the picture to the side. My experience in code heavily mirrors my development as an individual, as it has been with me in all the time I belive I have come to become who I am today.

  In my career as a developer, I have been in many projects, including some releasable video games, and more. Of the things I've coded, my experiments into Neural Networks and Genetic Algorithms are both things that I am very proud of. Down below is one game I developed with a team of 2 other developers, and 3 artists which I spearheaded, called Grapple. The premise of the game was that you were an astronaut, stuck in space and all you had to get around was a grapple gun. By shooting asteroids, you would pull yourself around a scene, dodging enemies, as well as dealing with the physics of pulling an asteroid towards you that had the potential to do some serious damage. On a side note, I also created the music for the game!
